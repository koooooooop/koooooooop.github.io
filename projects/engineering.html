Engineering Principles: Digital & Analog Circuits, PCB Design, and LiDAR Development

Published: 2024-05-16 Category: Engineering Principles Author: Zhipeng Zhou

This article details my project experience and technical achievements in digital circuits, analog circuits, PCB design and manufacturing, and LiDAR embedded development. Through these projects, I systematically mastered the entire process from circuit principles to practical hardware implementation, laying a solid foundation for subsequent embedded system development and hardware design.

I. Digital Circuit and Analog Circuit Design

In digital circuit and analog circuit courses, I completed multiple experimental projects covering basic circuit principles and advanced circuit design techniques:

1.1 Digital Circuit Projects

  • Designed and implemented an 8-bit counter based on 74-series chips, with reset and hold functions
  • Developed a digital clock circuit, implementing time display and setting functions
  • Used FPGA to implement a simple state machine for a traffic light control system
  • Completed the design and implementation of a digital frequency meter with a measurement accuracy of ±0.1Hz

1.2 Analog Circuit Projects

  • Designed and built multi-stage amplifier circuits with adjustable gain and low distortion
  • Implemented various filter circuits, including low-pass, high-pass, and band-pass filters
  • Developed signal conditioning circuits based on operational amplifiers
  • Designed a voltage-stable power supply circuit with adjustable output voltage and ripple coefficient less than 1%

II. PCB Design and Manufacturing

In hardware design, I mastered every aspect of PCB design and personally participated in the board manufacturing and soldering process:

2.1 PCB Design Tools and Skills

  • Proficient in using Altium Designer for schematic design and PCB layout
  • Mastered multi-layer board design technology, including power layer and ground plane planning
  • Understood and applied high-speed signal routing principles, such as impedance control and differential pair routing
  • Able to perform circuit layout and shielding design according to EMC/EMI requirements

2.2 Soldering Techniques

  • Proficient in using soldering iron for through-hole components and SMD components
  • Mastered soldering techniques for complex packages such as BGA and QFN
  • Able to use hot air guns and soldering stations for component removal and rework
  • Understood and applied lead-free soldering process standards

2.3 Practical Project Case

I completed the design and manufacturing of a multi-functional sensor interface board with the following features:

  • Integrated multiple ADC conversion channels, supporting analog signal acquisition
  • Provided various communication interfaces including SPI, I2C, and UART
  • On-board power management circuit, supporting multi-voltage power supply
  • Reserved expansion interfaces for convenient function extension

III. LiDAR Embedded Development

In the embedded systems direction, I focused on the application and development of LiDAR technology:

3.1 LiDAR Working Principles

I deeply studied ToF (Time of Flight) and triangulation laser ranging principles, comparing the differences between different technologies in terms of accuracy, distance, and anti-interference capabilities. I understood the working mechanisms and signal processing workflows of pulse LiDAR and phase LiDAR.

3.2 Hardware Design and Interfaces

  • Designed driver circuits and signal conditioning circuits for LiDAR sensors
  • Implemented an STM32 microcontroller-based data acquisition system
  • Developed interface modules for communication with host computers, supporting USB and serial communication
  • Integrated motor control circuits to implement LiDAR scanning functionality

3.3 Software Development

  • Wrote low-level driver programs to control laser ranging modules
  • Developed data processing algorithms for point cloud filtering and feature extraction
  • Implemented a simplified version of the SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) algorithm
  • Developed a Qt-based host computer software for data visualization and parameter configuration

3.4 Project Results

Based on the above work, I successfully developed a small LiDAR system with the following features:

  • Ranging from 0.1-10 meters with an accuracy better than ±2cm
  • 360° horizontal scanning angle with a 30° vertical field of view
  • Scanning frequency up to 10Hz, with the ability to collect 1000 points per scan
  • System power consumption less than 5W, suitable for mobile robot platforms

IV. Summary and Outlook

Through practicing these engineering principle projects, I not only mastered skills in circuit design, PCB manufacturing, and embedded development but also honed system thinking and the ability to solve complex engineering problems. In the future, I plan to further research the application of deep learning in LiDAR point cloud processing, explore more efficient SLAM algorithms, and attempt to apply the system in autonomous driving and intelligent robotics fields.
