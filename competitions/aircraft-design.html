Aircraft Design Competition

Innovation at the intersection of aviation and artificial intelligence

Competition Overview

The China University Aircraft Design Innovation Competition represents one of the most prestigious aeronautical engineering challenges for university students across the country. This annual event brings together top engineering talents to design, build, and test innovative aircraft solutions that push the boundaries of aeronautical engineering.

As a participant, I was responsible for developing visual algorithms, implementing AI hardware solutions, integrating the flight control system, and manufacturing fixed-wing components. This multidisciplinary approach allowed me to apply theoretical knowledge to practical engineering challenges while collaborating with a team of dedicated individuals.

Our team during the final presentation day of the competition

Challenge Description

Competition requirements and evaluation metrics

The competition challenged teams to design and build an autonomous fixed-wing aircraft capable of completing a series of complex missions:

  • Autonomous takeoff and landing in designated areas
  • Navigation through predefined waypoints with high precision
  • Real-time object detection and classification
  • Data transmission to ground control station
  • Operations under varying weather conditions

Teams were evaluated based on flight performance, technical innovation, system reliability, weight efficiency, and presentation quality. The competition emphasized not only the technical aspects but also the practical applications of the design in real-world scenarios.

Technical Solution

Our solution integrated advanced computer vision, embedded AI, and aerodynamic design to create a high-performance autonomous aircraft capable of complex mission execution.

Computer Vision System

Our vision system utilized a multi-stage approach:

  • YOLOv5 model optimized for aerial perspective object detection with 92% accuracy
  • Custom TensorRT implementation reducing inference time to 24ms per frame
  • Kalman filter-based tracking algorithm for maintaining target lock during occlusion
  • Adaptive contrast enhancement for varying lighting conditions

Embedded AI Hardware

The computational core of our aircraft featured:

  • Jetson Nano with custom cooling solution supporting sustained 15W operation
  • Quantized neural networks (INT8) for 3.2x inference speedup
  • Dedicated image signal processor for pre-processing camera feeds
  • Custom power delivery circuit with dynamic frequency scaling

Flight Control System

Our control system achieved precise navigation through:

  • Modified PX4 firmware with custom PID control loops
  • Hybrid sensor fusion algorithm combining GPS, IMU, and visual odometry
  • Multi-level failsafe system with autonomous emergency landing capabilities
  • Predictive path planning algorithm for obstacle avoidance

Aerodynamic Design

The airframe was optimized through computational fluid dynamics and iterative testing:

  • Custom airfoil design with 18% improved lift-to-drag ratio compared to conventional profiles
  • Carbon fiber composite construction reducing weight by 32% compared to traditional materials
  • Optimized motor and propeller configuration for maximum thrust efficiency
  • Modular design allowing for quick field repairs and component replacement

The final design achieved a balance between flight stability, payload capacity, and endurance that exceeded competition requirements.

Software Integration

The software system featured a modular architecture for reliability and extensibility:

  • ROS-based middleware for inter-module communication with guaranteed message delivery
  • Hierarchical state machine for mission management and fault recovery
  • Custom ground control station with real-time telemetry visualization
  • Automated testing framework for continuous validation of flight algorithms

This software architecture enabled rapid development iterations while maintaining system stability and safety.

Implementation Details

System Architecture

Our aircraft utilized a layered architecture with clear separation between flight control, perception, and decision-making systems:

  • Perception Layer: Camera and sensor fusion systems for environmental awareness
  • Processing Layer: Jetson Nano running optimized CV algorithms and flight planning
  • Control Layer: PixHawk system with customized PID controllers for flight stability
  • Communication Layer: Redundant telemetry systems with fail-safe protocols

We employed a modular design approach that allowed for quick iterations and component testing without affecting the entire system.

Implementation Challenges

Several technical challenges required innovative solutions:

  • Weight Distribution: Carefully balanced the aircraft to maintain center of gravity within optimal range despite different payload configurations
  • Heat Management: Designed custom cooling solutions for the Jetson Nano to prevent thermal throttling during compute-intensive operations
  • Power Optimization: Implemented dynamic power management to extend flight time while ensuring compute resources for critical tasks
  • Signal Interference: Redesigned the PCB layout to minimize EMI between high-frequency digital systems and analog sensors

Iteration and Refinement

Throughout the development process, we embraced an iterative approach to continually improve our design and performance. Each testing phase revealed new insights that led to significant refinements:

  1. Aerodynamic Optimization: Wind tunnel testing revealed inefficiencies in our initial wing design, leading to a redesign that improved lift-to-drag ratio by 18%.
  2. Computer Vision Accuracy: Initial field tests showed poor object detection in varying lighting conditions, prompting algorithm improvements and dataset augmentation that increased detection accuracy from 76% to 92%.
  3. Power System Efficiency: Battery performance analysis led to a complete redesign of the power distribution system, extending flight time by 35%.
  4. Weight Reduction: Structural analysis identified redundancies that allowed us to reduce the aircraft weight by 12% without compromising strength.

Technical Achievements & Results

Vision System Performance

Our computer vision system achieved remarkable results in various testing scenarios:

  • Object Detection: 92.4% mAP at 0.5 IoU threshold across 8 target classes
  • Processing Speed: 24ms average inference time (41.6 FPS) on Jetson Nano
  • Memory Efficiency: 68% reduction in model size through quantization without accuracy loss
  • Reliability: Maintained 87% detection rate under challenging lighting conditions

The system successfully detected and classified objects at distances up to 120 meters, providing ample time for trajectory adjustments.

Flight Control Performance

The flight control system demonstrated exceptional precision and reliability:

  • Navigation Accuracy: Maintained position within ±2 meters of planned waypoints
  • Wind Tolerance: Stable flight in crosswind conditions up to 15 km/h
  • Landing Precision: Autonomous landing within 1.5 meters of target point
  • Flight Time: 45 minutes of continuous operation on a single battery charge
  • Recovery Rate: 100% successful recovery from simulated sensor failures

  • Power Efficiency: Dynamic power management extended flight time by 35% compared to initial prototypes
  • Thermal Management: Maintained AI compute hardware below 65°C even under full load
  • Communication Range: Reliable telemetry and video transmission up to 5km line-of-sight
