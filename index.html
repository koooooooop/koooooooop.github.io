About Me

I'm Zhipeng Zhou, born on October 18, 2004, and currently a sophomore majoring in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics Engineering (Moon X Innovation Class) at the National School of Excellence in Engineering, Chongqing University. I have a strong interest in software development and technological innovation, with a current focus on research in artificial intelligence and deep learning.

Currently, I am focused on learning and research in Artificial Intelligence. I have been guided by Professor Sun Jianing and am now conducting research under the supervision of Professor Fan Qilin. I am also undertaking a research internship at the Tsinghua Shenzhen International Graduate School.

My research interests primarily include Sparse Inference Acceleration of Large Models, 3D Vision-Language Models (3DVLLM), Time-varying Workload Prediction, and Machine Learning & Deep Learning Algorithms.

Publications

Who Detects Better? A Comparative Study on Misinformation Detection by Humans and Large Language Models
Zhou, Z., Liu, X., He, H., Ding, D., Qi, Q.
Proceedings of the Annual Meeting of the Cognitive Science Society (2025)

This study investigates how LLMs and humans react to misinformation by analyzing their performance across five categories of errors: intellectual, common sense, reasoning, misleading, and logical errors. Our findings reveal distinct response patterns between LLMs and humans, with LLMs generally achieving higher accuracy but lacking depth in reasoning-based assessments.

Zhou, Z., et al.
AAAI Conference (2024)

This temporal model research presented at the AAAI conference combines Mamba state space models with Mixture of Experts (MoE) for high-precision prediction of time-varying loads, achieving leading results in multiple benchmark tests.
